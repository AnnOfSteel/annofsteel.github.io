KEY ART

THROUGH THE LOOKING-WINDOW

Anthony is a curious traveller peeking into a vibrant room in Asia–Pacific, filled with fascinating artefacts. Framed by a window, he stands as an outsider – a symbol and proxy for discovery and cultural exchange – offering a look into the world of Asian and South Pacific artistry, creativity, innovation, and craftsmanship. Outside the window are glimpses of a beautiful countryside. Inside, the composition showcases a rich blend of traditional and contemporary objects, including intricate handmade items such as pottery, sculptures, and textiles. I created this concept to appeal to enthusiasts of art, interior design, homewares, collectibles, and travel.

Art direction, including the curation of bespoke furniture and decorative objects. PHOTOSHOP: Key art concept mockup, compositing, and final retouching.

KEY ART

LAST MAN STANDING

For the final season of Mad As Hell, a political satire show, I developed a key art concept that addressed several elements. The source material consisted of individual photos of Shaun Micallef in a cowboy outfit. I leveraged this imagery to create a commentary on gun culture, while also capturing the show’s signature zaniness. The concept paid homage to previous seasons’ key art by featuring multiple Shauns, reminiscent of Agent Smith from The Matrix.

KEY ART

GARDENING AUSTRALIA

For past seasons of Gardening Australia, the show used basic cast photos with a logo overlay.

I tackled the challenge of being supplied uninspiring, brown-toned and non-professional photos of Costa Georgiadis, developing a more dynamic concept combining it with stock vegetation imagery. My goal was to create a hyperrealistic image where Costa appeared truly immersed in nature, almost as if he were becoming one with it. However, to maintain the show’s lifestyle genre, I kept his casual clothes visible and highlighted the natural textures around him. The producers were thrilled with the final result!
